Step 1: Rent collection

Who collects rent from the tenants?

When property managers collect rent...
Invoices and Payments Flow

Because you collect rent from tenants, it means that payments will be due from you to the landlord. Rentalr will track rent payments that you collect from tenants, subtract how much is owed to you for property management fees (per the contract terms that will be specified below), and expect you to send on the rest of the rent to the landlord. Invoices will be generated according to the contract schedule, and will indicate money owed to the landlord. You will be able to send online payments to the landlord.

Adding Payment Transactions

When an invoice is generated, once you pay the landlord then you will need to add a Transaction and set the Type to "Payment to Landlord". This will update the amount paid on the invoice.

When Landlords Collect Rent...
Invoices and Payments Flow

Because the landlord collects rent from tenants, it means that payments will be due from the landlord to you. Rentalr will track property management fees due (per the contract terms that will be specified below), and expect the landlord to send those fees to you. Invoices will be generated according to the contract schedule, and will indicate money owed to you. The landlord will be able to send online payments to you.

Adding Payment Transactions

When an invoice is generated, once the landlord pays you then you will need to add a Transaction and set the Type to "Property Management Fees Received". This will update the amount paid on the invoice.

Step 3: Contract Terms

Next we need to set up the landlord's contract terms, which Rentalr uses to generate invoices for payments due from the landlord in the past as well as going forward. For example, if you set the landlord as paying $100 monthly and set the contract start date to 3 months ago, Rentalr will generate 3 invoices with the amount due set to $100, with a total amount due of $300. After a month goes by, Rentalr will generate another invoice and the total due will increase to $400.

Next we need to set up the landlord's contract terms, which Rentalr uses to generate invoices for payments owed to the landlord in the past as well as going forward. For example, if you set the landlord as paying $100 monthly, set the contract start date to 3 months ago, and collect $1,000 of rent from the landlord's tenants each month, Housters will generate 3 invoices with the amount due set to $900, with a total amount due of $2,700. After a month goes by, Rentalr will generate another invoice and the total due will increase to $3,600.

Step 4: Invoicing

Invoices are used by Housters to track how much rent is due by a tenant and how much was paid. If you set a lease start date that is in the past, Housters will auto-generate invoices on each day that rent was due, based on the lease terms. On each future day that rent is due, a new invoice will be generated.

Enter a description for the invoices that are auto-generated, otherwise we'll call them "Payment due from {start date} to {end date}".
